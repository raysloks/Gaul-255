Hit Points

Your maximum number of hit points is equal to your level plus your Brawn and Guts. If your hit points become equal to or lowet than zero, you lose consciousness.

Hit Point Floor

Your hit point floor starts out at as your hit point maximum. If you lose hit points and your new amount of hit points is lower than your hit point floor, your hit point floor changes to your new amount of hit points. If your hit point ever becomes equal to or lower than the negative of your maximum hit points, you die.

Resting

Short Rest

Your hit point floor is increased by half of your maximum hit points, and you use the Rest action as much as is allowed.

Long Rest

Your hit point floor and current hit points are set to your maximum hit points.

Hemorrhage

Lesser Hemorrhage

At the start of their turn, a creature with a flesh wound must make a Guts check against the wound's level. On a failure, the creature's current hit points and hit point floor are reduced by 1. On a fumble, the level of the wound is increased by 1. On a critical, the wound is disabled.

Hemorrhage

At the start of their turn, the creature's current hit points and hit point floor are reduced by half the level of the wound.

Greater Hemorrhage

At the start of their turn, the creature's current hit points and hit point floor are reduced by the level of the wound.

Knockout

The creature loses consciousness for a number of rounds equal to half the level of the wound, the level of the wound is then equal to the number of rounds of unconsciousness remaining. The creature also gains a Concussion of the same level.
